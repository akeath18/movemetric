Reference Freshness Checker (DOCX → finds older in-text citations and suggests newer sources)

Upload a .docx file, set a cutoff year, and the app will flag in-text citations older than the cutoff (e.g., (Smith, 2012) or Smith (2012)). For each flagged spot, it extracts nearby context and queries Crossref for newer candidate references.

Tip: This uses a client-side DOCX-to-text converter (Mammoth).
Crossref prefers a contact email in the User-Agent.

Privacy note: The DOCX is processed locally in your browser. Only small keyword queries (not your full paper) are sent to Crossref to retrieve suggested references.

What counts as a “citation” here? (click)
This demo looks for common APA-style in-text years:
  • Parenthetical: (Author, 2010), (Author & Author, 2010), (Author et al., 2010)
  • Narrative: Author (2010)
It won’t perfectly parse every style or complex citation cluster. You can expand it if you need MLA/Chicago or multiple years.
